Acer Spin 311-2H CP311-2H-C7QD Convertible Touchscreen Chromebook

Operating System: Chrome OS
Display: 11.6" IPS Touchscreen LED-backlit (1366x768)
Dimensions (WxDxH): 11.42" x 8.11" x 0.74"
Touch Screen: Yes

Connectivity: 2x2 802.11ac WiFi 5, Bluetooth 4.2

The Acer Spin 311-2H CP311-2H-C7QD is a flexible and practical choice for those seeking a Linux-based operating system laptop in the form of a Chromebook. Designed with everyday digital tasks in mind, it offers a robust combination of features that cater to users who prioritize cloud computing, online learning, and media consumption.

Key Features

  • Display & Graphics: The laptop features an 11.6" HD (1366x768) IPS Touchscreen that presents a clear and vibrant viewing experience. Equipped with ARM Mali-G72 MP3 Graphics, it supports smooth visuals for everyday tasks. The included capacitive stylus pen enhances its touch capabilities, making it a convenient tool for note-taking and design tasks.
  • Processor & Performance: Powered by the Intel 4-Core Celeron N4000 Processor, clocked at 2.0GHz, coupled with 4GB of LPDDR4X RAM, it ensures efficient multitasking. Although not suited for heavy gaming or demanding software, it comfortably handles everyday applications and browsing.
  • Storage & Connectivity: With 64GB of eMMC storage, it provides adequate space for essential apps and files. Additional storage or backup can be easily managed through cloud solutions thanks to its Chrome OS. For connectivity, it includes 1 x USB Type-C port, 1 x USB 2.0 port, a 3.5mm combo jack, and a Kensington Lock Slot, supporting versatile connection options.
  • Operating System: Running on Chrome OS, it benefits from seamless integration of thousands of apps, automatic updates, and built-in cloud features, reinforcing its position as a capable device for modern digital workloads.

This Chromebook's lightweight build and responsive touchscreen design make it particularly appealing for students and professionals in need of a portable, efficient, and secure computing solution.
