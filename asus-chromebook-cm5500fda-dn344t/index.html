The ASUS Chromebook Flip CM5 (model CM5500FDA-DN344T) is a robust and adaptable device designed for users who seek a Linux-compatible platform with powerful hardware and user-focused flexibility. Released in 2021, this Chromebook stands out with features that are both practical and sophisticated.

Key Specifications

  • Processor and Graphics: Powered by the AMD Ryzen 3 3250C CPU with two cores and a clock speed ranging from 2.6 GHz (base) to 3.5 GHz (turbo), it offers a solid performance for multitasking. The integrated AMD Radeon Graphics ensures smooth visuals for everyday tasks.
  • Memory and Storage: Packed with 4GB of DDR4 RAM and 64GB of eMMC storage, this Chromebook supports basic productivity and casual use.
  • Display: The 15.6-inch touchscreen Full HD 1920x1080 NanoEdge display provides a sharp and immersive viewing experience. Thanks to the durable 360-degree hinge, users can easily flip the display into tent, stand, or tablet mode.
  • Connectivity and Ports: Staying connected is seamless with Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) and Bluetooth 5.0. The device also features a versatile selection of ports.
  • Build and Design: Sporting a sleek Mineral Gray aluminum chassis, the Chromebook weighs 4.3 lbs and has dimensions of 14.08 x 9.48 x 0.73 inches. Its backlit keyboard enhances usability in low-light environments.

A Great Companion for Linux

While shipped with Chrome OS, the ASUS Chromebook Flip CM5 is increasingly popular among Linux enthusiasts. Whether you're dual-booting or replacing Chrome OS entirely, the AMD Ryzen processor and Linux-friendly hardware make it a capable device for productivity and development.

Who Is This Laptop For?

This Chromebook is ideal for users who require a portable, touchscreen-enabled device with robust build quality. It suits students, professionals, and casual users who value versatility and are looking for a lightweight yet capable Linux-compatible laptop.

Final Thoughts

The ASUS Chromebook Flip CM5 blends performance, adaptability, and modern connectivity into one sleek device. For those venturing into Linux on Chromebooks, the CM5 provides a solid foundation without compromising on essential features.
