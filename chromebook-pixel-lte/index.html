Chromebook Pixel LTE

Brand: Google
Operating System: Chrome OS
Display: 12.85" High Resolution IPS (2560x1700)
Dimensions (WxDxH): 11.72" x 8.84" x 0.64"
Touch Screen: Yes
Weight: 3.35 lbs
Processor: 1.8 GHz Intel Core i5
Memory: 4 GB DDR3 SDRAM
Storage: 64 GB SSD
Battery life: 5 hours
WLAN: Wi-Fi 802.11a/b/g/n
Colors: Silver
Input Devices: Keyboard, Touchpad, Microphone, Video Camera
Connectivity: USB, SD Card reader

The Chromebook Pixel is an unusual laptop in several respects, it has a 12.85-inch IPS display with a maximum resolution of 2560x1700 pixels and an extra-wide viewing angle of 178° and it is by far the most expensive laptop shipped with Chrome OS to date.

The LTE model of the Chromebook Pixel is powered by a 1.8 GHz dual-core Intel Core i5 processor. It comes with 4GB RAM, 64GB SSD, 2 x USB 2.0 ports, 2-in-1 card reader supporting SD and MMC, Bluetooth 3.0 and a battery that offers up to 5 hours of continued use. The recommended retail price of the LTE version was US $1,449 at the time the Pixel was released.

When you listen to what people like about Chromebooks one aspect mentioned frequently is that they are low-cost and easy to replace with another Chromebook if they break or get lost. Obviously the low-cost part doesn't apply for the Pixel and unsurprisingly it is outsold by many other Chromebooks, still it has quite prominent proponents.
