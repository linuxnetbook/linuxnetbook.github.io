ASUS CX1500CKA Chromebook Review

If you're in the market for a budget-friendly Chromebook, the ASUS CX1500CKA might have caught your eye. This 15.6-inch device promises basic functionality at an affordable price. In this review, we'll dive into the details of its performance, build quality, and overall value to help you decide if it's the right choice for you.

Build and Design

The ASUS CX1500CKA has a plasticky feel, with a smooth top and a rough bottom that might aid grip. Weighing in at 3.95 pounds (1.75 kg), it's relatively heavy for its class. However, it offers a decent array of ports, including dual USB-C ports, a headphone jack, and a Micro SD card slot, making it versatile for various connectivity needs.

Keyboard and Trackpad

The keyboard on this Chromebook is a mixed bag. While the keys are well-spaced and include a dedicated number pad, the keyboard itself suffers from some flex when pressure is applied. The trackpad is functional but not exceptional, with occasional issues in recognizing two-finger taps for right-clicking.

Screen Quality

One of the most significant drawbacks of the ASUS CX1500CKA is its screen. Although it boasts a Full HD resolution, the TN panel results in washed-out colors and poor viewing angles. The screen can tilt back 180 degrees, but it's not a convertible device, which might be a deal-breaker for some users.

Performance and Battery Life

Powered by the Intel Celeron N4500 processor, this Chromebook handles general use and light gaming, such as Roblox, quite well. The battery life is another plus, easily lasting into the second day with light use. The speakers and webcam are average, aligning with the overall budget nature of the device.

Conclusion

The ASUS CX1500CKA Chromebook is a solid choice for those seeking a basic, budget-friendly laptop. While it has its shortcomings, particularly with the screen and keyboard flex, it offers decent performance and battery life for everyday tasks. If you're looking for an affordable Chromebook and can overlook its minor flaws, the ASUS CX1500CKA might be worth considering.
